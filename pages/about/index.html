I am a Mechanical Engineering student at the Colorado School of Mines with a minor in Robotics and Intelligent Systems, currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Robotics Engineering. My work focuses on distributed robotics and resilient multi-agent systems. At the MPALA Lab, I develop and test aerial swarm platforms using Crazyflie drones, OptiTrack motion capture, and ROS 2 to study consensus and resilient control algorithms under real-world conditions.

Background & Interests

My academic interests are centered around multi-agent robotic systems, though I’m generally curious about most areas of engineering. I spent all of high school as the lead programmer for my school’s robotics team, so when it came time to choose a major, computer science felt like the obvious path. At the same time, I’ve always enjoyed working with my hands and understanding how things are built. I didn’t want my work to live entirely behind a screen. Choosing mechanical engineering gave me the chance to develop the intuition and practical background needed to design robotic systems, not just program them. I’ve especially enjoyed SolidWorks modeling and finite element analysis because they let me turn abstract ideas into physical designs and test assumptions before anything is built.
