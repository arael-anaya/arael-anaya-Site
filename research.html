My research has spanned designing and simulating resilient consensus protocols (such as W-MSR), tuning Kalman filters for localization accuracy, and translating theoretical models of multi-agent coordination into experimental demonstrations. I enjoy the challenge of bridging rigorous mathematical analysis with hands-on implementation, refining flight dynamics, and debugging distributed systems to make autonomous swarms robust against uncertainty and adversarial interference.

Contributed to research on multi-robot teleoperation and AI-driven robotics systems with a focus on communication, coordination, and decision-making. Integrated multiple laboratory robots using ROS to enable collaborative experiments and data collection. Enhanced multi-teleop interfaces with DIARC, refining usability, responsiveness, and experimental performance. Developed and tested an AI-powered robotic assistant that leveraged machine learning to improve adaptability and decision-making in real time.Co-authored a research paper on robotics and multi-agent systems (publication pending), contributing to technical writing and academic dissemination.
